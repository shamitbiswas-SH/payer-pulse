Regulatory & Government Watch

HHS Notice of Benefit and Payment Parameters for 2027 (Final Rule). This is the annual rulebook for the ACA marketplaces, finalizing 2027 payment parameters, updates to the HHS-operated risk adjustment and risk adjustment data validation (HHS-RADV) programs, and user fee rates for QHP issuers on Federally-facilitated and State-based Exchanges. Lines of business: ACA/Exchange. Issuers should already be aligning pricing, risk-adjustment data practices, and fee assumptions for plan year 2027. (source ↗)

Medicaid Community Engagement Requirement (Interim Final Rule w/ comment). This IFC implements the new statutory Medicaid community-engagement (work) requirement, setting expectations for which applicants and beneficiaries must demonstrate engagement as a condition of eligibility. States must implement no later than January 1, 2027. Lines of business: Medicaid. Expect significant enrollment volatility, eligibility-verification workflow changes, and member-communication demands for Medicaid managed care plans. (source ↗)

Medicaid Managed Care State Directed Payments (Proposed Rule). CMS proposes alternatives to the cap on the total payment rate and other requirements governing state directed payments in managed care, citing its authority over prepaid inpatient and ambulatory health plans. Lines of business: Medicaid. Because directed payments are a major channel for funneling supplemental dollars to providers through MCOs, any change to the rate ceiling directly affects MCO contracting and provider economics. (source ↗)

Vacatur of Parts of the 2024 Section 1557 Nondiscrimination Rule (Notice). A federal court vacated certain provisions of the May 2024 Nondiscrimination in Health Programs and Activities rule, specifically where they expanded obligations (the vacated portions tied to expanded coverage requirements). Lines of business: ACA/Exchange, Medicaid, Medicare Advantage, commercial. Plans and covered entities should review benefit-design and nondiscrimination policies that were built to the 2024 standard. (source ↗)

Nationwide Enrollment Moratoria — Hospices and Home Health Agencies (Notices). CMS imposed 6-month nationwide moratoria on new Medicare enrollment of hospices and of home health agencies, effective May 13, 2026. Lines of business: Medicare Advantage, Medicaid. Network adequacy in affected post-acute/home-based categories may tighten, and the move signals continued program-integrity scrutiny in these provider segments. (source ↗)

IOTA Organ Transplant Model Updates (Final Rule). CMS finalized updates to the Increasing Organ Transplant Access (IOTA) Model for Performance Year 2, plus a technical correction. Lines of business: Medicare. Limited direct payer impact, but relevant for plans tracking transplant-related value-based and access initiatives. (source ↗)
